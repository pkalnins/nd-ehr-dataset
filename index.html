About the ND-EHR Dataset

The ND-EHR Dataset was assembled as part of a methods-feasibility pilot funded by RAND REACH / NCCIH (U24 Pilot Grant) and conducted at the Helfgott Research Institute, National University of Natural Medicine. De-identified retrospective EHR data were extracted from seven community naturopathic clinics in Washington State using the FHIR R4 API, de-identified using Google Cloud Healthcare API and Data Loss Prevention, and loaded into a structured cloud-based research database (Google BigQuery).

The ND-EHR Dataset supports population characterization, treatment-pattern analysis, and longitudinal outcome analyses. Treatment orders were extracted from clinical notes using a validated keyword classification system (positive predictive value 93.3%, 95% CI: 90–96%) and categorized into 12 naturopathic treatment modalities.

The study was approved by the NUNM Institutional Review Board (IRB #CK021726). All clinics executed data sharing agreements prior to data extraction.

Data Access

The Helfgott Research Institute at NUNM is the sole gatekeeper for secondary researcher access to the ND-EHR Dataset. The dataset is a HIPAA Limited Data Set (45 CFR §164.514(e)) — access requires an executed Data Use Agreement (DUA).

To request access: Submit your research question to Helfgott/NUNM at the address below, along with your institutional affiliation and IRB status (existing approval, or a request for an IRB reliance agreement).

Access Process

  1. 1Submit a research question to Helfgott/NUNM for scientific and ethical vetting
  2. 2Obtain IRB approval — through your home institution (Helfgott/NUNM issues an IRB reliance agreement) or through NUNM's IRB directly
  3. 3Execute a Data Use Agreement with the Helfgott Research Institute; data fees apply
  4. 4Receive access: read-only BigQuery credentials for self-service analysis, or request assisted analysis by the project's data consultant
  • Self-service access is provided as read-only credentials to the ND-EHR Dataset in Google BigQuery. Data download to local storage is not permitted under the DUA.
  • The Institute for Natural Medicine (INM) is acknowledged on resulting papers and presentations but does not participate in access vetting or governance.

Treatment Categories

Treatment orders were extracted from plan sections of clinical notes and classified into 12 naturopathic treatment modalities. Three additional categories are captured as clinical practice metrics.

sleep_hygieneSleep hygiene recommendations and behavioral interventions for sleep

Data Dictionary

The ND-EHR Dataset is hosted in Google BigQuery and comprises 11 tables plus derived views. Connection details are provided upon authorization.

Core Tables

TableKey FieldsNotes
patients patient_id, birth_year, birth_sex, race, ethnicity, clinic_id Use birth_sex (not gender) — ChARM miscodes ~18K patients as "other" in the gender field. Race/ethnicity 89.1% unknown, reflecting documentation practices.
encounters encounter_id, patient_id, encounter_date, encounter_type, clinic_id Dates are shifted per patient (±0–364 days); within-patient intervals are preserved but absolute calendar dates are approximate.
conditions condition_id, patient_id, encounter_id, icd10_code, description, onset_date Problem list is carried forward to every encounter — always use the conditions_unique view for prevalence analysis.
observations observation_id, patient_id, encounter_id, loinc_code, test_name, value, unit, obs_date 3.6M records. Use standardized_observations view for normalized metric names and curated LOINC matching (30–50% of records exported without codes).
clinical_notes note_id, patient_id, encounter_id, note_type, note_text, note_date 858,394 plain-text notes (CarePlan + DocumentReference). PHI redacted via Google DLP and rule-based passes.
medication_requests med_request_id, patient_id, encounter_id, medication_id, instructions, prescribed_date ND-initiated prescriptions only. Free-text entry; not normalized to RxNorm.
medications medication_id, drug_name Lookup table for drug names referenced in medication_requests.
treatments treatment_id, patient_id, encounter_id, note_id, category, keyword_matched, is_order, source_text NLP-extracted rows. Filter is_order = TRUE for clinician treatment orders (406,731 rows). Extracted from plan sections only.
clinical_note_sections section_id, note_id, section_type, section_text SOAP section segmentation: subjective, objective, assessment, plan, patient_instructions.
cohort_elevated_hba1c patient_id, first_elevated_hba1c, hba1c_value, glycemic_group, treatment_count Pre-built cohort: HbA1c ≥5.7% + ≥1 treatment order (n=2,965).
cohort_longitudinal_hba1c patient_id, measurement_count, first_hba1c_date, last_hba1c_date, follow_up_days Pre-built cohort: ≥2 HbA1c readings (n=5,576).

Derived Views

ViewPurpose
conditions_unique Patient-level deduplicated conditions. Always use this view for prevalence analysis — raw conditions inflates counts due to problem-list carry-forward.
standardized_observations Adds normalized metric_name and curated LOINC matching for labs and vitals, handling records exported without standard codes.
Key data quality notes: Encounter dates are shifted per patient (±0–364 days) — cross-patient calendar-time analyses are not supported. Clinic 1 has minimal lab data (providers did not enter results into ChARM). Race/ethnicity is 89.1% unknown. Medication names are free-text and not normalized to a standard vocabulary. Cross-clinic patient duplication was not corrected; patients seen at multiple clinics appear as distinct records.
